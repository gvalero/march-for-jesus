7. PR Messaging & Context

The media landscape has changed since 2025. Last year, controversy drove coverage. This year, we must create our own narrative. Three defensive/strategic angles establish credibility:

  • Traffic Apology (proactive): Lead with an advance apology for disruption. This paradoxically attracts media interest & shows professionalism.
  • No Conflict with Palestinian Parade (reassurance): Explicit, calm messaging that both events have been coordinated. De-escalates potential controversy pre-emptively.
  • Growth & John's Leadership: Emphasise 16–20,000 expected attendance (vs. 8,000 last year). John Ahern as organiser & Europe coordinator. Reference Dublin's 25,000 to show scale.

Key insight: Press releases are distribution. Social media is where we win — and that means vibrant, joyful, family-friendly content that stops the scroll. Institutional media (TV, radio, print) are follow-on; social drives momentum.

8. Practical Notes

Production

  • Shoot vertical 9:16 by default. One clip can serve IG Reels, TT and YT Shorts.
  • Burn captions in — most people watch on mute.
  • Keep a shared Drive/Dropbox folder of approved b-roll, logo files, lower-third graphics.

Voice & tone

  • Warm, family-friendly, non-political — same as the website.
  • Always include: date (16 May), location (Belfast), call to action (link in bio).
  • Hashtags: #MarchForJesus #MarchForJesusBelfast #Belfast2026 — keep it tight.

Approvals

  • One person owns the calendar & final approval — avoid posting-by-committee.
  • Sensitive content (testimonies, minors) — written consent before posting.

The one rule

If a post or release doesn't help someone register, steward, attend or invite — it doesn't go out this fortnight. Save it for after the march.
