Brand & Resource Guide

March for Jesus Europe

This page is intended as a private reference for March for Jesus organisers across Europe. It brings together the approved brand direction, the city naming recommendation and the Belfast planning documents that other cities can adapt locally.

The central aim is consistency. If marches across Europe stay close to the same visual language, the movement will feel more established, more recognisable and more clearly connected from city to city.

Questions or file requests: information@marchforjesus.co.uk

What this guide covers

  • The approved logo direction for public-facing use.
  • The recommended way to add city names to the mark.
  • The core brand palette and typography direction.
  • The Belfast planning documents available for download.

Core palette

Colour palette

The approved brand direction centres on burgundy and cream, supported by plum, charcoal, black and white. These colours give the identity warmth, consistency and strong contrast across digital and print applications.

How to use this pack

Use this guide as a shared foundation, then tailor the operational documents to your city while keeping the visual identity as aligned as possible with the approved March for Jesus brand direction.

This page is intended for direct sharing rather than public navigation. Please circulate it only with relevant organisers, local leadership teams and approved delivery partners.
