March for Jesus Belfast Advance Traffic Notice: Careful Planning to Minimise Disruption on 16 May 2026

March for Jesus UK and Ireland apologises in advance for temporary traffic disruption in central Belfast on Saturday 16 May as thousands gather to celebrate faith and unity.

The annual March for Jesus Belfast will draw an estimated 16,000–20,000 participants from churches across Ireland and beyond. The organisers acknowledge the impact on local traffic flow and wish to thank the public, local businesses, and Belfast City Council for their patience and cooperation.

"We recognise that a public gathering of this scale creates temporary challenges for traffic and local residents," said John Ahern, organiser of March for Jesus Belfast. "Our team has worked carefully with council partners and traffic authorities to minimise disruption. We are grateful for the professionalism and support we have received."

Event Details:

• Date: Saturday 16 May 2026
• Start: 10:00 AM gathering at Ormeau Park (opposite Ozone Complex, BT6 8LT)
• March departure: 2:00 PM from Ormeau Park
• Route: Ormeau Embankment into Belfast city centre to City Hall
• Conclusion: 3:00–5:00 PM at Belfast City Hall (BT1 5GS)

The march is family-friendly, non-political, and open to all ages and denominations. Parking is available at Ormeau Park (downloadable permit required). Full route details and FAQs are available at marchforjesus.co.uk.

March for Jesus Belfast is organised by March for Jesus UK and Ireland in partnership with churches across the region. The event celebrates Christian faith through worship, prayer, and public testimony to exalt the Name of Jesus Christ in the heart of Belfast.
