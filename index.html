SCHEDULE

10:00AM - 2:00PM

ORMEAU PARK GATHERING (BT68LT)

Please join us in the area opposite the Ozone Complex in Ormeau Park! The day begins with a relaxed time of community and fellowship with friends and families before the march starts. Enjoy a welcoming atmosphere with a variety of food trucks and refreshments and a time to connect with others and build community.

2:00PM - 2:50PM

MARCH BEGINS

The march will officially begin, departing from the fields opposite the OZONE COMPLEX of Ormeau Park and proceeding down the ORMEAU EMBANKMENT into the city towards Belfast City Hall. We will walk together in unity, lifting up the Name of Jesus!

3:00PM - 5:00PM

BELFAST City Hall EVENT (BT15GS)

The event will conclude with a gathering in front of Belfast City Hall, featuring a time of worship, testimonies and corporate prayer for the city and the nation. All attendees are encouraged to stay and take part in this powerful gathering of Christians from across the land!

MARCH ROUTE

The march will move from Ormeau Park to Belfast City Hall. Please review the route before event day.

FREQUENTLY ASKED QUESTIONS

Who can participate?

Everyone is welcome! People of all ages, churches, and denominations can join the march. Families, youth groups, and individuals are all encouraged to attend.

Is there a fee to participate?

No, participation is free! However, there will be an opportunity to donate on the day as well as online donations.

How many people attended in 2025?

Over 8,000 people attended March for Jesus Belfast in 2025, and over 25,000 attended March for Jesus Dublin in 2025, making it one of the largest Christian public gatherings in Ireland.

What is March for Jesus?

March for Jesus Belfast is a large-scale, non-political, family-friendly public event celebrating the Christian faith. Organised by Christian Voice Ireland, it brings together churches and Christians from across the land in worship, prayer, and public testimony to exalt the Name of Jesus Christ in the heart of Belfast. The 2026 event takes place on Saturday 16th May.

What is the march route?

The march departs from Ormeau Park (opposite the Ozone Complex, BT6 8LT) at 2:00pm, proceeds along the Ormeau Embankment into Belfast city centre, and arrives at Belfast City Hall (BT1 5GS) for worship and prayer from 3:00pm to 5:00pm.

Will there be toilets at City Hall?

Yes, there will be multiple portable toilets in the vicinity of City Hall.

Are there accommodations for the disabled or elderly?

There will be a limited seating area in front of the stage designated for the disabled and the elderly. Please arrive at City Hall from 2.30pm to ensure seating.

Will there be health and safety measures in place?

Yes, trained stewards will be present throughout the march. Qualified ambulance paramedics will be onsite to provide medical assistance if required.

Where can I get food and refreshments?

There will be food trucks at the Ozone Complex of Ormeau Park. At City Hall there are a variety of cafes and shops.

Will there be parking at Ormeau Park?

You will need to download a parking permit and print it off, displaying it on your windshield on the event day. Parking is only permitted inside the park; parking elsewhere is not allowed. Download Parking Permit
